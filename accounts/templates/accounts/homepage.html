Jonah's Story

This a picture of a man. That man’s name is Jonah. Jonah loves learning languages. Jonah speaks five languages and has spent extended periods of time in different countries, meeting people and practising his languages. Jonah has loved every minute of his experiences, however, one element of his travels has always been particularly challenging: making lasting friendships. Now, I know what you are thinking; maybe Jonah is a werido. This is possible, however, as far as we can tell, Jonah is quite normal (apart from his obsession with language-learning). The simple truth is that people are often so busy with their own lives that they don’t have time to make friends with travelling weirdos like Jonah. This is where we come in.

Language learning is preocupied with giving students a base in a language, think Duolingo, Memrise, Italki. Language Stay represents the next step in the road to proficiency.

The Concept

Our aim is to facilitate the process of making target-language-speaking friends by putting you right in the heart of a family or household. You will live, work, eat and, most importantly, converse(!) with your host(s) everyday and, before long, you’ll be nattering away in Finish or French or Farsi as part of the family. Gone are the days of spending inordinate sums on language courses abroad only to learn from a textbook and speak English with the other foreign students or of spending days off wandering around with no-one to talk to!

The Deal

You can sign up either as a “host” or as a “worker”. A subscription to this website as a “worker” will allow you to make a profile, search through host “offerings” and contact the hosts with whom you would like to stay. A subscription as a “host” will allow you to create an “offering” and to search for and contact workers who you think would be a good fit for your residence. Generally, a worker would be required to work a certain number of hours per day in exchange for meals and lodging (provided by the host). A host must also bear in mind that the principal reason for the worker’s stay is to learn the language, so including the worker in the day-to-day rhythms of the family is paramount (mealtimes, games, trips).
